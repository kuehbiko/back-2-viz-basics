### Description
For Week 4 of 2026, we would like you to explore the Shape card.

### Summary
**Challenge:** This week, we are focusing on the Shape property on the Marks card. Shapes are a simple yet powerful way to add visual context to your data, allowing you to move beyond basic bars and lines. By using icons or symbols, you can make your visualizations more intuitive and engaging for the viewer. This is a foundational skill for anyone looking to add a layer of design and clarity to their dashboards.

**Data:** The 2026 Winter Olympics in Milano-Cortina have just come to an end! This week’s dataset contains the final medal standings for the participating nations. Your task is to use the Shape card to represent the data, whether you are categorizing by country or by medal type. Our data fields are:

**Country:** The name of the country \
**NOC:** Three-letter country code \
**Gold:** Number of gold medals won \
**Silver:** Number of silver medals won \
**Bronze:** Number of bronze medals won \
**Total:** The overall medal count

**For those newer to #B2VB:** Focus on the basics of assigning shapes. Try changing your mark type to Shape and dragging a dimension onto the Shape shelf. Experiment with the default shape palettes in Tableau to see how different symbols can represent your data points clearly.

**For more experienced users:** Challenge yourself by using custom shapes! Try adding your own icons such as medals, flags, or winter sports symbols to your Tableau Repository. Focus on how these custom marks can enhance the storytelling of the Olympic results without cluttering the view.
