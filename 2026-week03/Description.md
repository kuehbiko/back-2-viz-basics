### Summary
**Challenge:** This week, we’re focusing on Dot Plots, a clear and effective way to compare values across categories. Dot plots help highlight differences and patterns by placing values along a shared scale, making it easier to see how items relate to one another at a glance.

They are especially useful when you want to emphasize ranking, spread, and gaps across categories while keeping the visual simple and uncluttered.

Data: This week’s dataset looks at pet populations (Cats and Dogs) across multiple countries. Your task is to use a dot plot to explore how pet ownership varies by country and between pet types. Our data fields are:

**Country:** The name of the country \
**Flag Code:** Country identifier \
**Pets:** Type of pet (Cats or Dogs) \
**Population:** Number of pets 

**For those newer to #B2VB:** Focus on the fundamentals of a clear dot plot. Pay attention to alignment and spacing so comparisons across countries are easy to follow. Be intentional with labels and color, keeping the view readable and free from unnecessary distractions.

**For more experienced users:** Think about how you can add clarity and emphasis to the story. You might highlight notable differences between countries, guide attention with annotations, or use design choices that make patterns stand out without overwhelming the viewer.
